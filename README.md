## **Network Routes Explained — 9929, CMIN2, CN2 GIA, IIJ, BGP**

These acronyms get thrown around a lot in VPS coupon articles without anyone explaining what they actually mean. Let me fix that.

**CN2 GIA** — China Telecom's premium global internet access line. This is the highest-quality route for China-bound traffic. It's a dedicated path that avoids the congestion and variable latency of standard international transit. If you're serving Chinese users, this is what you want. It's also the most expensive, which is why the "China Premium Optimised" plans cost more.

**9929** — China Unicom's AS9929 premium route. Similar concept to CN2 GIA but on the Unicom side. Provides stable, low-latency connectivity for China Unicom users.

**CMIN2** — China Mobile International Network 2. The premium international route for China Mobile traffic. Combined with 9929 and CN2 GIA, these three cover the big three Chinese ISPs with premium routing on each.

**BGP** — Border Gateway Protocol. This is standard internet routing — nothing special, just the normal way traffic finds its way around. "BGP Network, China Optimised" means they've done some route optimisation but it's not the premium CN2/9929/CMIN2 tier.

**IIJ** — Internet Initiative Japan. One of Japan's most reputable upstream network providers. The Osaka plans route through IIJ, which gives them excellent connectivity within Japan and to the broader Asia-Pacific region.

The practical takeaway: if you see "GIA & 9929 & CMIN2" in a plan description, that's the premium China routing trifecta. If you see just "9929 & CMIN2," you're getting two of the three. If you see "BGP, China Optimised," it's optimised but not premium-tier. And if it just says "International network," there's no China-specific optimisation at all — that's fine for global workloads, just don't expect great China latency.
